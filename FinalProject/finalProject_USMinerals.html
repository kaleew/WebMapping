Trump's Attack on Environmental Justice

In the first months of Donald Trump's second presidency, he signed over 100 executive orders. Many of these are levers for dismantling the federal government in such a way that serves his personal agenda. Executive Order (EO) 14241: "Immediate Measures to Increase American Mineral Production" completely alters the approach to energy projects and mineral extraction. The purpose of the EO is to fast-track selected projects (identified by Trump's own National Energy Dominance Council). By reframing previous environmental regulations as unnecessary and burdensome, Trump's adminstration can essentially override regulatory barriers and avoid accountability for the impacts of these projects.


Rather than prioritizing a commitment to cleaner energy, the Trump administration is setting the industry back significantly. In addition to energy, other extractive mining projects focus on product manufacture, like lithium batteries, for example. Under the guise of creating jobs and shifting priorities from energy renewability to "reliability," this EO sacrifices the health of the environment and climate-susceptible communities in favor of "making American energy dominant" and allegedly reducing reliance on foreign nations.


EO 14241 threatens tribal sovereignty, especially in environmental governance and land stewardship. By bypassing much of the assessment process and denying appeals made by Tribal Nations, the National Energy Dominance Council and the federal government have effectively taken Indigenous ecological knowledge out of the equation. These projects have the potential to seriously harm ecosystem functions and populations of people, non-human animals, vegetation, and water supplies. Any small steps that had been made previously, notably by the Biden administration, are on the way to being completely undone by this and similar executive orders.
